Let me help make your academic, reference, and nonfiction books a little easier to understand. I create comprehensive back-of-the-book and embedded indexes, allowing your readers to identify and find key topics within the text.

When creating an index, I first read your work and extract the important terms and subjects, balancing a desire to be thorough with an understanding that no reader wishes to dig through dozens of pages to track down the phrase they are searching for. Once I have created a preliminary list of topics, I take to Sky Index and the Chicago Manual of Style to produce a formatted, embedded index. From there, I will shape your index based off of your preferences.

About Me

Barbara Murphy combines her academic and personal expertise to create accurate, professional and timely back-of-the-book as well as embedded indexes. She obtained her Masters of Science degree in Plant, Soils and Environmental Science from the University of Maine in 1992 and went on to a 23-year career as an associate professor with the University of Maine Cooperative Extension. There, she focused on horticulture, agricultural sciences, hunger and poverty. Barbara also has hands-on experience in small-scale organic vegetable production as well as organizig successful community gardens. She brings decades of experience in teaching, both with students and adults. In 2015 she successfully completed the University of California Berkeley course, Indexing: Theory and Application.
