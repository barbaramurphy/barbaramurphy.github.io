Let me help make your academic, reference, and nonfiction books a little easier to understand. I create comprehensive back-of-the-book indexes, allowing your readers to identify and quickly find key topics within the text.

I thoroughly enjoy indexing. I love being exposed to a wide variety of ideas and topics as well as the challenge of converting an author's perspective into reader- friendly index entries.

I have created indexes on an eclectic mix of subjects ranging from biodiversity in gardens to the natural history of dinosaurs. My specialties and interests are in the agricultural sciences (botany, gardening and landscaping, local food movements) as well as issues concerning education, hunger and poverty, health, and current social/political movements.

My indexing work is professional, thorough, and reader-friendly. I understand the time constraints in publishing, and always meet deadlines.

About Me

Barbara Murphy combines her academic and personal expertise to create accurate, professional and timely back-of-the-book as well as embedded indexes. She obtained her Masters of Science degree in Plant, Soils and Environmental Science from the University of Maine and was an associate professor with the University of Maine Cooperative Extension for 23 years. Barbara also has hands-on experience in small-scale organic vegetable and specialty plant production as well as organizing community efforts.

When she is not indexing, she is part of Gardeners Growing Healthy Communities. They are a volunteer group of gardeners and cooks who believe sharing garden harvests promotes health and thriving communities. Click here to learn more.
